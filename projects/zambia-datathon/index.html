ZAMBIA — Zenith AI for Mapping & Browsing International Agreements on Climate Action

Harvesting the power of AI to bring more clarity around national climate strategies and policies.

App currently under reconstruction

Core Idea

  • Browse and summarize national climate policy documents (often PDFs) and compare countries on key topics.
  • Rely on Retrieval‑Augmented Generation (RAG) to ground answers in the underlying documents.
  • Provide concise, comparable outputs so policymakers and researchers can see similarities and differences quickly.

Tech Stack

  • RAG: hybrid retrieval over national strategy PDFs.
  • Models: DeepSeek‑R1‑7B, Llama‑3.2‑1B; aggregation with Perplexity API where helpful.
  • UI: topic‑wise exploration and country comparisons with similarity indicators.

Strategy to Bring Clarity

  • Create prompts for each topic and each country.
  • Show similarity of found policies per topic.
  • Enable side‑by‑side country comparisons on selected topics.

Prompt Templates — Summaries

  • Energy mix: Which renewable energy sources are planned to be the main contributors to the energy mix of {country}? What are their percentages and target year?
  • Carbon tax: Is {country} implementing or considering a carbon tax? If yes, what are the details?
  • Energy subsidy: Is {country} subsidizing certain forms of energy? If yes, which ones and how?
  • Green energy: Which green energies are {country} considering for the transition to net‑zero? Provide details.
  • Carbon capture: Is {country} considering or already using carbon capture? Provide details.
  • Private renewable investment: How much private investment is {country} relying on to achieve climate targets? Provide details.

Prompt Template — Comparison

Given two summaries, produce a ≤100‑word comparison in English:

Compare {country1} and {country2} on their {selected_topic} policy based on:
- {country1}'s {selected_topic} policy: {summary1}
- {country2}'s {selected_topic} policy: {summary2}
What are the key differences and similarities?

Data Sources

  • National strategies and policy documents in PDF format ingested into the RAG pipeline.
